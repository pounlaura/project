GHG emissions implications
NYC's annual emissions are 50.7 million metric tons.
Buildings are responsible for 70% of those emissions.
Covered buildings under LL84 emitted 19.5 million metric tons in 2018.

*estimated emissions from 25,164 covered buildings

LL97 aims to reduce at least 5.3 million metric tons by 2030 from covered buildings.






How much GHG emissions does each covered building have to reduce to meet LL97 goals?


Large gaps in data make it difficult to visualize LL97.

The city currently is missing GHG emissions data for about 40% of the covered buildings. We have estimated approximately 50,000 buildings will be covered by LL97.(source: Urban Green Council) However, we only have data from about 27,000 buildings.
The maps below show the building emissions data gap. The left map displays where the ~50,000 covered buildings are. The orange buildings are the buildings with no data. The right map displays the ~27,000 private covered buildings with data. Slide to see the significant data gap for building greenhouse gas emissions.
