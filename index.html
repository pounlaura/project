NYC's Green New Deal


In 2019, New York City (NYC) enacted Local Law 97 (LL97), an ambitious law that sets greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions reductions limits for NYC's buildings as part of the city's efforts to reduce GHG emissions by 80% by 2050. This new legislation sets limits on carbon emissions that come from energy use in large buildings. Starting in 2024, building owners will be fined if their buildings emit more than the designated emissions cap. We delved into the regulations and currently available emissions data to gain insights on what it means for NYC to achieve the law's goals.

Local Law 97:


  • Affects buildings greater than 25,000 square feet
  • Sets increasingly stringent limits on carbon emissions per square foot in 2024 and 2030
  • Flexibility to comply through renewable energy credits and/or emissions offsets
  • Allows some affordable housing to choose low-cost energysaving measures instead of emissions limits
  • Creates a new Office of Building Energy and Emissions Performance at Department of Buildings
  • Strong advisory board to help refine emissions metrics and limits
  • Carbon trading study and implementation plan
  • Penalties for non-compliance and variances for financial hardship
